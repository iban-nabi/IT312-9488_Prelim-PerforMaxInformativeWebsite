Human Resources is the department within a company that handles everything related to its employees. It can be further divided into many different fields, one of which is Performance Management.

What is Performance Management? The success of any company lies in the competence of its employees in achieving the company’s objectives. As such, it is essential that everyone is equipped to the best of their ability to handle their respective tasks. Performance Management ensures the capability of each employee by providing a system of feedback and communication between managers and their employees. This creates an environment of growth and development, guiding the learning and goal-setting process of the employees as they continue to work in the company.




HISTORY OF PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT


Performance management in human resources is familiar because we have heard it in the news and have been reading it in articles. The early 1900s was the beginning of performance management when more formal appraisal systems started to be adopted by a significant portion of businesses. Walter Dill Scott's system may have been the first performance appraisal recorded. However, it was widely accepted in the middle of the 20th century. In the 1960s, most performance appraisal systems were built to focus more on what a person might be able to accomplish in the future than on how competent they initially appeared to be. Self-evaluation has become more critical in recent years. Performance evaluations improved as the year went on by emphasizing goals and objectives more.

The year 1970s were different because, in these years, they were finding flaws, appraisals were carried out, came under heavy criticism, and the cases were brought before courts. Psychometrics and rating scales are also being incorporated in businesses due to how arbitrary and opinion-based most appraisal systems are. Over the following 20 years, from the 1980s to the early 2000s, employee engagement and motivation by businesses increased, producing a more in-depth approach to performance management. Companies introduced different and new metrics, which are still considered extremely important today.

Encouraging and motivating your people is essential to increase their productivity, reach their goals, and outperform rivals. Performance management systems promote a supportive environment for each employee's growth to achieve the company's objectives.
